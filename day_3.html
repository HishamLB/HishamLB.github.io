Day Three

For the third day of the routine you will be doing shoulders and forearms.

Shoulders:

Shoulders/delts are crucial muscles when it comes to overall upper body strength as they are connected to the chest and arms. The Deltoids, Teres Major and Rotater Cuff all come in mind when developing a great shoulder workout so they will be trained here.

The Delts/Deltoids are very important muscles that are responsible for everything to do with arm abduction, rotation, extension and all of that.They also help stabilize the entire shoulder joint. These muscles are very crucial to train as they provide immense strength to the body.
The Rotator cuff muscles are one of the most hated muscles as they limit trainers in other excercises so often. These muscles work with the arm and shoulder joint to provide mobility and internal rotation of the arm. They can be a big bottleneck in compound excercises like the bench press so it is crucial to train them.
The Traps/Trapezius muscles are responsible for elevating the shoulder blade and tilting it when the arm is moved. Even though technically speaking the traps are in the back their function is heavily related to the shoulder and so they are here. They move, stabilize and rotate the scapula. These are the muscles that work the most when we shrug.

The workout:

  • Shoulders:
    • Barbell Overhead Press
    • Reverse Pec Fly
    • Cable Face Pulls
  • Forearms
    • Barbell Reverse Curls
    • Hammer Curls

Barbell Overhead Press (3 sets of 15)

Even though this excercise works more than just your shoulders, that will be the case with a lot of these since the way your muscles work is in conjunction.

How to perform the Barbell Overhead Press
  1. Load up a barbell with appropriate weights then stand with your feet shoulder width apart.
  2. Hold the barbell at your shoulder level then push the bar upward and squeeze your shoulder blades together at the top of the movement
  3. Lower the barbell back at shoulder level and repeat.

Reverse pec fly (4 sets of 12)

This excercise utilizes the pec deck machine and is perfect to build shoulder muscles. Read any warnings/instructions on the machine.

How to do Reverse Pec Flys
  1. Reverse a pec fly machine and after you set the weight appropriately hold the handles with your palms facing inward.s
  2. Tighten your torso then extend both your arms all the way.
  3. Slowly return to the starting position then repeat for the number of reps+sets.

Cable face pulls (4 sets of 15)

Face pulls are a stable of any shoulder excercise as they utilize the shoulder delts really well.

How to perform Cable Face Pulls
  1. Stand facing the cable machine and set the cables around eye height.
  2. Attach a rope to the machine then grip it with both arms and raise up your arms to shoulder height.
  3. Tuck your elbows in and pull the cable to your face, hold for 3 seconds while engaging your back and core then slowly release and go back to starting position.

Forearms:

Forearms are very overlooked when it comes to workout routines but it's really important for fleshing out your arms and not introducing any bottlenecks to your strength and physique. There's 3 main muscles in the forearm and they are the Brachioradialis, extensors and flexors. Training these muscles will help build large strong forearms.

Barbell reverse curls (4 sets of 15)

We have already talked about normal barbell curls in the bicep excercies on day 2 but now we are reversing it to target the extensor muscles. This excercise is very important to build forearm muscles and so we will be utilizing it in this workout.

How to perform Barbell Reverse Curls
  1. Start with a barbell loaded with weights 10 pounds lighter than what you would use for a normal barbell curl.
  2. Stand up with feet hip-width apart and grab the barbell with an overhand grip then slowly curl the barbell up slighty above 90 degrees.
  3. Slowly return to the starting position and repeat.

Hammer curls (4 sets of 12)

Next you are going to do hammer curls which heavily utilize all 3 muscles we mentioned at the start, which are very important if you perform any sports where throwing is a thing. To do this excercise hold the dumbbells by your side with neutral wrists. Next curl up the weights upwards and inwards towards your chest. Repeat for 4 sets of 12 reps.
